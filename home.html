About Pakistan

Pakistan, officially the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, is a country in South Asia. It has a rich history, vibrant culture, and a resilient spirit that has inspired the world. From the majestic peaks of the Karakoram to the bustling streets of Karachi, Pakistan is a land of diverse landscapes and people, united under one flag.

Our National Heroes

Quaid-e-Azam

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan, played a pivotal role in the creation of Pakistan. His leadership and vision continue to inspire the nation.

Allama Iqbal

Allama Iqbal, known as the Poet of the East, inspired the idea of a separate homeland for Muslims in the Indian subcontinent through his powerful poetry and philosophy.

Abdul Sattar Edhi

Abdul Sattar Edhi, the founder of the Edhi Foundation, is revered for his humanitarian work. His legacy of selfless service to humanity lives on in Pakistan and beyond.

Abdul Qadeer Khan

Abdul Qadeer Khan, known as the father of Pakistan's nuclear program, played a pivotal role in making Pakistan a nuclear power by leading the development of the country's first atomic bomb.

Events & Celebrations

Independence Day

Every year on August 14th, Pakistanis celebrate their independence with patriotic fervor, marked by flag hoisting ceremonies, parades, and cultural performances.

Pakistan Day

On March 23rd, Pakistan Day commemorates the Lahore Resolution and the adoption of the first constitution of Pakistan with military parades and national pride.

Eid ul-Fitr

Eid ul-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan, a joyous occasion celebrated with family gatherings, special prayers, and festive meals across the country.

Defence Day

Observed on September 6th, Defence Day honors the armed forces' sacrifices during the 1965 war, with ceremonies and tributes across the nation.
